**Question 1** *(Source: 2014W-MEC-511-E-NS-Sample.pdf, Part A.1 Q1)*
In a static fluid which is incompressible, two points in the fluid at different depths:
- A) Have the same pressure head.
- B) Have the same pressure.
- C) Have the same piezometric head.
- D) Have the same elevation head.

**Answer**: C
**Explanation**: In a static fluid, the piezometric head (pressure head + elevation head) remains constant at all points. At different depths, pressure changes but the sum of pressure head and elevation head is constant.

- B) At the centroid of the submerged area.
- C) At or above the centroid of the total area depending on the size of the area.

### 1.3 THERMODYNAMICS - THEORY (2012W & 2013W Exams)

**Question 9** *(Source: 2012W-MEC-511-E-NS-Sample.pdf, Question 1, Part I)*
Which of the following is not true about internal energy?
- A) Internal energy equals the sum of kinetic and potential energies for a closed system.
- B) Internal energy is a part of total energy, E.
- C) Internal energy is a microscopic form of energy.
- D) Internal energy is an extensive property.

**Answer**: A
**Explanation**: Total energy E = U + KE + PE (macroscopic). Internal energy U ≠ KE + PE. Internal energy is a microscopic form due to molecular motion, is part of total energy, and is extensive (depends on mass).

Which of the following is not true about work?

- C) Work transfer is a property of a system.
- D) A little bit of work done by a system undergoing a volume change can be expressed as δW = p dV where p = pressure and V = volume.

**Answer**: C
**Explanation**: Work is a process quantity (like heat), NOT a property. Work depends on the path taken, not just initial and final states.

(b) The final temperature, T₂ (in kelvin)
(c) The work, W (in kJ), done during the process
(d) The heat, Q (in kJ), transferred during the process

**Solution**:

**(a) Final Volume V₂**:
For polytropic process: p₁V₁^n = p₂V₂^n

100 × (1.2)^1.5 = 600 × V₂^1.5
100 × 1.317 = 600 × V₂^1.5
131.7 = 600 × V₂^1.5
V₂^1.5 = 0.2195
V₂ = (0.2195)^(1/1.5) = (0.2195)^(0.667) = **0.367 m³**

**(b) Final Temperature T₂**:
Using ideal gas law:
p₁V₁/T₁ = p₂V₂/T₂

100 × 1.2 / 317.5 = 600 × 0.367 / T₂
0.378 = 220.2 / T₂
T₂ = 220.2 / 0.378 = **583 K**

**(c) Work Done W**:
For polytropic process: W = (p₂V₂ - p₁V₁)/(1 - n)

W = (600 × 0.367 - 100 × 1.2) / (1 - 1.5)
W = (220.2 - 120) / (-0.5)
W = 100.2 / (-0.5)
W = **-200.4 kJ**

(Negative indicates work done ON the system - compression)

**(d) Heat Transfer Q**:
From First Law: ΔU = Q - W
Q = ΔU + W

ΔU = m × cv × (T₂ - T₁)
ΔU = 2 × 0.657 × (583 - 317.5)
ΔU = 1.314 × 265.5 = **349.0 kJ**

Q = 349.0 + (-200.4) = **148.6 kJ**

(Positive indicates heat transfer INTO the system)

### 3.2 THROTTLING CALORIMETER (2014W Exam - Part C.2)

**Question 36** *(Source: 2014W-MEC-511-E-NS-Sample.pdf, Part C.2)*

**Problem Statement**:
A group of students perform an experiment to measure the steam quality in a supply pipe. The steam line carries a two-phase mixture of steam at a pressure of 6 bar (gauge). A small fraction of the flow in the pipe is diverted through a throttling calorimeter and exhausted to the atmosphere where the atmospheric pressure is 1 bar (absolute). The temperature of the exhaust steam is measured as 120°C.

**Required**: Answer the following questions:
(a) What is the value of the supply pipe pressure, and its units, that must be used to obtain the steam properties inside the supply pipe?
(b) What is the temperature, T (in °C) of the steam inside the supply pipe?
(c) What is the enthalpy, h (in kJ/kg) of the steam exiting the calorimeter?
(d) What is the percent steam quality, x%, inside the supply pipe?

**Solution**:

**(a) Supply Pipe Pressure**:
Gauge pressure = 6 bar
Absolute pressure = 6 + 1 = **7 bar** (must use absolute pressure for steam tables)

**(b) Temperature Inside Supply Pipe**:
At 7 bar saturation: T_sat ≈ 164.9°C
Since steam is two-phase at 7 bar, the temperature is the saturation temperature:
**T = 164.9°C** (or rounded to ~165°C)

**(c) Enthalpy at Exit of Calorimeter**:
Exit conditions: p₂ = 1 bar (absolute), T₂ = 120°C
At 1 bar: T_sat ≈ 99.6°C
Since T = 120°C > T_sat, steam is superheated

From superheated steam tables at 1 bar and 120°C:
**h₂ = 2706.7 kJ/kg** (or ~2707 kJ/kg)

**(d) Steam Quality in Supply Pipe**:
Throttling is isenthalpic: h₁ = h₂ = 2706.7 kJ/kg

At 7 bar saturation:
- h_f = 697.22 kJ/kg
- h_g = 2763.5 kJ/kg
- h_fg = 2066.3 kJ/kg

Quality: x = (h₁ - h_f) / h_fg = (2706.7 - 697.22) / 2066.3
x = 2009.5 / 2066.3 = 0.973 = **97.3%**

### 3.3 CONTINUITY EQUATION - UNSTEADY FLOW (Chapter 4 Problems)

**Question 37** *(Source: MEC511-Suggested-Problems-Solutions-04.pdf, Question 10 - Unsteady Flow)*

**Problem Statement**:
If the water level varies in a tank and V₂ = 6 m/s, find the rate of change dh/dt if the tank diameter d = 95 cm.

Given:
- D₁ = 0.05 m (inlet), V₁ = 4 m/s
- D₂ = 0.07 m (outlet), V₂ = 6 m/s
- Q₃ = 0.01 m³/s (volumetric flow rate at inlet 3)
- Tank diameter = 0.95 m
- ρ_water = 1000 kg/m³

**Solution**:

**Mass Balance (unsteady)**:
dm_tank/dt = ṁ_in - ṁ_out

**Inlet mass flow rates**:
ṁ₁ = ρ × V₁ × A₁ = 1000 × 4 × π(0.05)²/4 = 1000 × 4 × 0.001963 = 7.85 kg/s

ṁ₃ = ρ × Q₃ = 1000 × 0.01 = 10 kg/s

Total inlet: Σṁ_in = 7.85 + 10 = 17.85 kg/s

**Outlet mass flow rate**:
A₂ = π(0.07)²/4 = 0.003848 m²
ṁ₂ = 1000 × 6 × 0.003848 = 23.09 kg/s

**Mass accumulation**:
dm_tank/dt = 17.85 - 23.09 = -5.24 kg/s

**Water level change**:
dm_tank/dt = ρ × A_tank × dh/dt

-5.24 = 1000 × π(0.95)²/4 × dh/dt
-5.24 = 1000 × 0.7088 × dh/dt
-5.24 = 708.8 × dh/dt

**dh/dt = -0.00739 m/s = -7.39 mm/s**

(Negative indicates water level is dropping)

### 3.4 BERNOULLI APPLICATION - SIPHON (Chapter 3 Problems)

**Question 38** *(Source: MEC511-Suggested-Problems-Solutions-03.pdf, Problem 3.48)*

**Problem Statement**:
A 50-mm-diameter plastic tube is used to siphon water from the large tank as shown. If the pressure on the outside of the tube is more than 30 kPa greater than the pressure within the tube, the tube will collapse and the siphon will stop. The maximum height from the water surface is 2 m, and the siphon exit height is 4 m below the surface.

If viscous effects are negligible, determine the minimum value of h allowed without the siphon stopping.

**Solution**:

The lowest pressure occurs at the highest point in the tube (at height 2 m above water surface).

Apply Bernoulli from water surface (point 1) to top of tube (point 2):
p₁/(ρg) + V₁²/(2g) + z₁ = p₂/(ρg) + V₂²/(2g) + z₂

With p₁ = 0 (atmospheric), V₁ ≈ 0, z₁ = 0, z₂ = 2 m:
0 = p₂/(ρg) + V²/(2g) + 2

p₂/(ρg) = -2 - V²/(2g)

For collapse to not occur: |p₂| < 30 kPa = 30,600 Pa
-ρg × h_min = -30,600
h_min corresponds to pressure difference of 30 kPa

At the exit (4 m below surface):
Using continuity and Bernoulli from exit back to surface:
0 = 0 + V²/(2g) + 4 - (4 - h)
V²/(2g) = h - 4

For minimum h (maximum ΔP):
30 × 10³ = ρg(2 + h - 4)
30 × 10³ = 9810(h - 2)
3.06 = h - 2
**h_minimum ≈ 2.94 m**

Determine the hydrostatic force and location (center of pressure) on a vertical rectangular gate 2 m wide and 1.5 m tall that is submerged in water with its top 1 m below the surface.

## SECTION 5: SAMPLE EXAM - LONG PROBLEMS

### 5.1 COMPRESSOR POWER CALCULATION (2012W Exam)

**Question 43** *(Source: 2012W-MEC-511-E-NS-Sample.pdf, Question 4)*

**Problem Statement**:
Air is compressed at steady state from p₁ = 100 kPa (abs), T₁ = 295 K to T₂ = 439 K at a mass flow rate of 3 kg/s. The compression process is such that pv^1.27 = constant. During the compression process, heat is transferred from the casing to the surroundings at a rate of 90 kW.

Air properties: R = 0.287 kJ/(kg·K), cp = 1.005 kJ/(kg·K), cv = 0.718 kJ/(kg·K)
Flow velocities: V₁ = 40 m/s, V₂ = 110 m/s
Potential energy effects can be neglected.

**Required**: Calculate:
(a) The air pressure p₂ (in kPa absolute) at the exit
(b) The inlet air density ρ₁ (in kg/m³)
(c) The volume flow rate at the compressor inlet (in m³/s)
(d) The enthalpy change h₁ - h₂ (in kJ/kg)
(e) The kinetic energy change KE₁ - KE₂ (in kJ/kg)
(f) The power required to drive the compressor Ẇ (in kW), including kinetic energy effects

**Solution**:

**(a) Exit Pressure p₂**:
For polytropic process: p₁v₁^1.27 = p₂v₂^1.27

Using ideal gas and polytropic relations:
T₂/T₁ = (p₂/p₁)^((n-1)/n)
439/295 = (p₂/100)^((1.27-1)/1.27)
1.487 = (p₂/100)^0.2126
(1.487)^(1/0.2126) = p₂/100
(1.487)^4.704 = p₂/100
p₂ = 100 × **5.93 ≈ 593 kPa**

**(b) Inlet Density ρ₁**:
ρ₁ = p₁ / (R × T₁) = 100,000 / (287 × 295) = 100,000 / 84,665 = **1.18 kg/m³**

**(c) Volume Flow Rate at Inlet**:
Q₁ = ṁ / ρ₁ = 3 / 1.18 = **2.54 m³/s**

**(d) Enthalpy Change**:
Δh = cp(T₂ - T₁) = 1.005 × (439 - 295) = 1.005 × 144 = 144.7 kJ/kg
h₁ - h₂ = **-144.7 kJ/kg** (negative since h₂ > h₁ during compression)

Or: |h₂ - h₁| = **144.7 kJ/kg**

**(e) Kinetic Energy Change**:
KE₁ - KE₂ = (V₁² - V₂²) / 2 = (40² - 110²) / 2 = (1600 - 12100) / 2
= -10500 / 2 = -5.25 kJ/kg

|KE change| = **5.25 kJ/kg**

**(f) Compressor Power**:
Steady-flow energy balance:
Q̇ - Ẇ = ṁ[(h₂ - h₁) + (V₂² - V₁²)/2]

-90 - Ẇ = 3 × [144.7 + 5.25]
-90 - Ẇ = 3 × 149.95
-90 - Ẇ = 449.85
Ẇ = -449.85 + 90 = **-539.85 kW**

Magnitude: **|Ẇ| ≈ 540 kW** (power INPUT required)

### 5.2 POWER CYCLE THERMAL EFFICIENCY (2012W Exam)

**Question 44** *(Source: 2012W-MEC-511-E-NS-Sample.pdf, Question 2)*

**Problem Statement**:
A system undergoes a power cycle consisting of four processes. Some of the values of the energy transfers and energy changes are given in the table. All units are in kJ. Kinetic and potential energy changes are negligible.

| Process | Q | W | ΔE |
|---------|----|----|-----|
| 1→2 | (A) | -100 | (B) |
| 2→3 | (C) | 0 | 400 |
| 3→4 | 500 | (D) | 200 |
| 4→1 | (E) | 600 | -600 |

**Required**:
(a) Fill in all remaining values (A) through (E)
(b) Calculate the thermal efficiency

**Solution**:

Using First Law: ΔE = Q - W

**Process 2→3** (W = 0):
ΔE = Q - W → 400 = Q - 0
**(C) = 400 kJ**

**Process 3→4**:
ΔE = Q - W → 200 = 500 - W
**(D) = 300 kJ**

**Process 4→1**:
Already given: Q = (E), W = 600, ΔE = -600
-600 = (E) - 600
**(E) = 0 kJ**

**Process 1→2**:
-100 = (B) - (-100)?? Let me reconsider...

For a cycle: ΔE_cycle = 0
ΔE = (B) + 400 + 200 + (-600) = 0
(B) = 0

So: 0 = (A) - (-100)
(A) = -100 kJ

**(A) = -100 kJ**
**(B) = 0 kJ**
**(C) = 400 kJ**
**(D) = 300 kJ**
**(E) = 0 kJ**

**Thermal Efficiency**:

Q_in = |Q| for all heat additions = |-100| + 400 + 500 = 800 kJ
(taking absolute values of positive heats)

Actually, looking at signs:
Process 1→2: Q = -100 (heat out)
Process 2→3: Q = 400 (heat in)
Process 3→4: Q = 500 (heat in)
Process 4→1: Q = 0

Q_in = 400 + 500 = 900 kJ
Q_out = 100 kJ
Q_net = 900 - 100 = 800 kJ

W_net = Q_net = 800 kJ (for cycle)

η_th = W_net / Q_in = 800 / 900 = **0.889 = 88.9%**

Or: η_th = 1 - Q_out/Q_in = 1 - 100/900 = 800/900 = **88.9%**
